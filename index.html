FACFaculty
Consulting

Sitting beside the subject-matter expert

Most faculty know their content thoroughly and have never been asked to adapt it for online or hybrid modalities. I translate expertise into a teachable shape: surfacing what learners actually struggle with, sequencing it, and protecting the instructor's voice while the structure does the heavy lifting.

The output is rarely a single document. It is a shared decision the expert can defend: what to cut, what to assess, where to spend their limited time. From there I provide the training and support that helps faculty run what we built, through launch and beyond.

Consult notesexcerpt
  • Name the one thing learners must be able to do
  • Find where understanding usually breaks
  • Cut content that does not serve the outcome
  • Decide the evidence of success up front
TPLTemplates

Reusable structure, not one-off pages

A good template encodes a decision once so it does not have to be re-litigated on every module. I build module blueprints, page patterns, and tagged inventories that keep a team of designers consistent across dozens of units without flattening the material.

Each template carries its own instructions, so the next person inherits the reasoning, not just the layout.

GDEGuides

Writing so the reader can act

Facilitator guides, learner orientations, and reference material that respect attention. I write to the moment of use: what the reader is trying to do, what could go wrong, and the single next step. Plain verbs, no filler, examples that demonstrate rather than decorate.

The test for any guide is simple. Can someone follow it without me in the room.

WFLWorkflows

AI as a labor-multiplier, never the author

I design production workflows where automation does the repetitive lifting and a human holds authority over anything that reaches a learner. Every step is auditable: what the model drafted, what a reviewer changed, and why. Speed without losing the paper trail.

The discipline is in the gates, not simply the content generation. The AI model proposes; a person decides.

Assurance

The two-pane review

My signature check runs in two panes at once: inline comments on the artifact itself, and a structured log that scores every issue by severity and tracks it to resolution. Nothing gets waved through, and nothing gets lost between rounds.

It also produces evidence. When scope or quality is disputed, the log is the record.

PBKPlaybooks

Turning a fire drill into a procedure

When a tool gets deprecated across ninety labs, or a new platform has to be identified, tested and implemented mid-development, the difference between chaos and a clean migration is a playbook. I document the decision tree, the client-facing message, and the internal roles and responsibilities so the work can continue without risking delivery time and budget margins.

A playbook is institutional memory that does not depend on any one person staying.

Migration playbooktooling change
  • Audit every affected asset, tag by effort
  • Choose replacements, map old to new
  • Decide what the client needs to see
  • Stage, verify, roll forward by cohort
CRSCourse
Design

Alignment from the start

Every choice in a course should trace back to what the learner needs to be able to do. I hold that chain end to end: objective to assessment to activity to asset, with the ADDIE backbone keeping the build honest and the Community of Inquiry keeping it human.

I design across online, hybrid, and on-ground delivery, for adult and working learners and for certification-aligned outcomes. When the alignment is right, the course feels effortless to the learner. That ease is the design.

02

How I work

A few commitments that hold across every engagement, regardless of subject or client.

Structure first

ADDIE as a backbone, not a cage

Deep analysis and design mapping happen before anyone builds a page. The framework keeps the work grounded; it never replaces judgment about the learner in front of you.

Authority stays human

AI multiplies labor, people own the outcome

I build auditable, human-in-the-loop workflows. The role of the model and the role of the person are written down, so the line of accountability is never blurry.

Evidence over assertion

Every claim of quality has a record

Repeatable review processes, tagged inventories, and deliverable trackers mean quality is something you can verify and quantify.

Project discipline

I run development like a multi-phase, collaborative workshop

Timelines, deliverables, and stakeholder communication are managed with transparency. Scope and risk get raised before they become problems.

Who it serves

Built for adult and working learners

Adult learning theory is the design constraint. The people in these courses are busy, motivated, and often non-traditional. The path has to be clear enough to follow at 10pm after a shift.

Never finished

Improvement is continuous and evidenced

Course feedback, learner performance, and quality metrics drive the next revision. A course at launch is a hypothesis; the data tells you where it was right and where it needs another pass.
